Admiral Yi's Timeline

  • 1545 - Born in Geoncheon-dong(present-day Inhyeon-dong), Seoul on March 8th.
  • 1572 - Applied for a military officer examination in August, but failed to pass examination.
  • 1576 - Finally passed the military officer examination in Febuary and placed in the third rank group, called Byeonggwa.
  • 1580 - Appointed naval officer titled Manho in Balpo in July, the beginning of his career in the Navy.
  • 1582 - Dismissed from the post of Manho in January, trapped in the false accusation of inspector Seo Ik.
  • 1587 - Appointed officer titled Manho in the border town Josanbo in January.
  • Also in charge of managing reserved land called Dunjeonin Nokdundo near Josanbo in August; this experience helped him manage the Navy in the future.
  • Demoted to and served as a commoner in August, trapped under false accusations from his superior Yi Il.
  • 1589 - Appointed officer titled Hyeongam in Jeongeup, Jeollado province, in December. Jeongeup was the smallest admin division of Korea during the Joseon Dynasty.
  • 1591 - Promoted to Naval Commander, titled Sugun Jeoldosa of the western part of Jeollado province on Febuary 13.
  • 1592 - Completed building the Geobukseonon (the Turtle Ship) in April 12.
  • The Imjin War, Japanese Invasion, started on April 13 of this year. Korean Land forces would continue being defeated until October 6.
  • Naval Forces however, underneath Admiral Yi, would devastate the Japanese Fleet despite the impossible odds commonly faced. It was during this year that Admiral Yi would develop the "Crane's Wing" tactics
  • Notable battles won on this year: Battles of Okpo and Happo on May 7, Battle of Jeokjinpo on May 8. Battle of Sacheon on May 29, first battle the Turtle Ship was employed. Battle of Dangpo on June 2, Battle of Danghangpo on June 5, and the battle of Yulpo on June 7. Battle of Hansando on July 8, Crane Wing formation created. Battle of Angolpo on July 10 and Battle of Jangrimpo on August 29. Battle of Hwajungumi, Dadaepo, Seopyeongpo, Jeoryeong and Busan on September 1.
  • 1593 - Commanded at the battle of Ungcheonpo, Febuary 6 to March 8. Blocked the strait of Gyeonnaeryang to stop Japanese forces from going to the west in May. Korean Naval Headquarters were moved from Yeosu to Hansando on July 14. Promoted to Commander-in-Chief of the Naval Forces of the South on August 15.
  • 1594 - Defeated the Japanese at the second battle of Danghangpo on March 4. Protested against Chinese General Tan Zongren's order on March 6 to not attack the Japanese. Attacked the Japanese at the first battle of Jangmunpo on September 29 and the second battle of Jangmunpo on October 4.
  • The reason for the cease-fire was due to the Japanese wanting to end the war, however Admiral Yi new this was nothing but the Japanese trying to decieve the Korean/Chinese forces.
  • 1597 - Arrested and taken to Hanseong on Febuary 26.Imprisoned on March 4. Released from prison on April 1 and served as a commoner under General Gwon Yul. Yi's mother died on April 13. Was reinstated as Commander-in-Chief of the Naval Forces on July 23. Admiral Yi would then defeat the Japanese in the battle of Eoranjin on August 28, Battle of Byeokpajin on September 7, and Battle of Myeongnyang on September 16. His son would die this year as well.
  • The Koreans put General Won Gyun in place of Admiral Yi, his incompetence was so great that almost the entire Navy was destroyed by the Japanese within a few months. He himself would within the bried period of his command.
  • 1598 - This year would mark the end of the Imjin War. Admiral Yi, with the help of the Chinese Navy, would fight against the Japanese Navy in the final battle of Noryang. The Japanese would be defeated, however Admiral Yi was killed by a stray bullet during this battle. The Japanese Emperor at the time, Toyotomi Hideyoshi would also die this year. This in combination with the Japanese's crushing defeat at the hands of Admiral Yi would force the Japanese to withdraw from Korea.
